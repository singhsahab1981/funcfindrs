February 18, 2023

FundFindr Team

Getting funding all depends on whether you have a good idea

Getting funding all depends on whether you have a good idea, an excellent plan and a great team. But to ensure success, it’s not just about the idea, plan and team; it’s about presentation. Grant writing can be a challenging process. So having a solution that makes applying for grants quicker, easier and more efficient is the key to your success! Having a grant professional to assist you and provide higher-quality applications is the best way to ensure your success. That way you can spend less time focusing on finding funding, writing applications and sourcing supporting documents, and spend more time growing your business!

So, how do you get started?

Check out our tips below to improve every stage of your FundFindrs Journey. Help us, help you!!

Step 1: Get the most out of your initial consultation

At FundFindrs, when we begin your customer journey, we will schedule a consultation meeting with your team where we will basically go through the general overview of grants, we have found for you, based on initial research. Here, we will need you to “de-brief” us about your organisation and what it is that you do so we can get a good gist of what to look for.

  • You will most likely need to answer the following questions:
  • What makes your solution/product/business novel or innovative?
  • Detail your businesses goals and key milestones.
  • Provide an overview of the key features of your business plan including goals, strategies, and projected outcomes for the future
  • Can you demonstrate how your business contributes to the local economy, including any environmental and social outcomes.
  • Outline the initiatives you have implemented within the workplace or business practices that makes your business unique.

As the owner of the business, no one will know the details of the business better than you so make sure to give us all the information you can (it will only lead to more potential grants for your organisation)!

Step 2: Be sure to go over everything with your Grant Writer

While keeping the above step in mind, we also need you to notify us of your goals for the funding, which is where you would like the money to be directed. This could be for a specific project, manufacturing, research and development or even technical support.

When thinking of activities, products or services to allocate funding to you will need to think of the following;

  • Background – Why do you need the grant/award?
  • Track record – How can we demonstrate that you are a good candidate?
  • Outcomes – How can we measure the success of the business/project?
  • Objectives – What will the grant help you do?
  • Timeline – How long will the project take?
  • Budget – How much will the project cost?

Proving our team with as much information and supporting documents, both sales-wise and financial, allows our team to better develop a competitive application for your business. Please let us know whatever you need the funds for, and we will make sure the funding body is aware of this. We ask for this because grant providers are very eager to know how their money will be used, which will only make your case stronger!

Step 3: Maintain a consistent communication channel

Well, this step is the most important (and the only one you will really need to worry about). During the time frame allocated according to your grants calendar, there will be extra things we may need from you. This includes financial or other supporting documents which are essential for the submission of a grant application. Hence, it is crucial to maintain a regular communication channel and supply us with information they need as it is all for your benefit!rt your journey with us today!
